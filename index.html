For every child who held on tight to something soft and never let go.

Love at First Sight

Hi, I am Pink the Bunny! I used to sit on this shelf,

until a little girl named Jessie found me and loved me more than I could have imagined!

Artist notes: I hope to make this page one where Jessie first meets Pink. I want to show the moment of love at first sight, where Jessie is drawn to Pink on the shelf and reaches out to hold her.

Our first day

She carried me all the way home, holding me like I might fly away.

Jessie and I did everything together. Like two peas in a pod!

Artist notes: I wanted to make this page be the beginning to a list of activities they did together. This one sepcifically I wanted to show them walking in together, with Pink always with Jessie. I also wanted to create a two peas in a pod animation or visual.

Everywhere Together

and even the backseat of her mom's pink van!

Every single meal

She brought me to every single meal.

Oatmeal splattered my ears. Ketchup dotted my paws. And something blue... we don't talk about that.

The hard nights

On the hard nights, she pulled me close.

Artist notes: I wanted to capture Jessie's vulnerability as she confides in Pink during a moment of fear. I plan on adding texture and detail to the blankets and surroundings to ground the scene and make it feel more real.

So close that I could feel her heartbeat.

Artist notes: I wanted to capture a close-up of this moment. Some fixes include details to the face and adding more to the background to show that they are under the covers.

The mud incident

One time, I fell in the mud. Mud caked my fur.

Still, Jessie picked me right back up

Artist notes: A small moment in which Jessie and Pink are both covered in mud, but Jessie still holds her as tight as ever.

The washing machine

One day, Mama put me in the washing machine.

But Jessie never left my side, sitting and never taking her eyes off me.

Artist notes: Similar scene to the left, but the addition of Jessie sitting in front of the washing machine with snacks

First Day of School

But one day, she picked up her school bag and walked out the door, without me.

Waiting

I waited and waited.

And worried thinking "what if she needed me?"

Artist notes: I am hoping to make these scene very empty, with the extra white space emphasizing Pink's loneliness and isolation while waiting.

Time Passing

I sat there, dreaming and waiting for the moment she comes back.

Artist notes: I want to make this a moment of nostalgia where Pink reflects on her memories with Jessie and waits for her to return.

Artist notes: I want this scene to show Pink's deep longing and emotional connection with Jessie. Maybe a visual of her heart aching to show the reader she is really missing her and waiting for her to come back.

Shes back!!!

Then I heard footsteps. Jessie burst through the door!

Artist notes: I want this scene to show the joy and relief Pink feels upon seeing Jessie return. I want emotions of excitement and thrill, but also warmth and comfort of being reunited with someone you've been waiting for.

Reunited

Jessie dropped her bag and ran to me.

I will love you forever

She held me close, just like the first day we met.

And I finally understood: love isn't being together every second. Love is always coming back.

About the author  ·  Back endpaper

Michael Khela is a pre-med student, looking to explore every aspect of his life. This book was inspired by a real pink bunny, and by all the children who know exactly what it means to carry something small and love it completely.
